Biography

Zhi Wang (S'10-M'14-SM'22) is currently an associate professor and associate dean of the Institute of Data and Information at Shenzhen International Graduate School, Tsinghua University. He is also the Director of the TSIGS – Shine Works InfleXion Lab (Joint Innovation Lab). He received his Ph.D. in 2014 and his B.E. in 2008, both from Tsinghua University. His research areas include distributed machine learning, multimedia networks, mobile cloud computing, and industrial foundation models with embodied intelligence. He has led NSFC Key Projects, NSFC General Projects, and major projects of the Ministry of Science and Technology, and was selected for the National High-Level Talent Special Support Program for Young Top Talent.

He was a recipient of the Natural Science Award of the Ministry of Education (First Prize) in 2017, the National Natural Science Award (Second Prize) in 2018, the Shenzhen Youth Science and Technology Award in 2019, the Technology Invention Award of the Chinese Institute of Electronics (First Prize) in 2020, and the Natural Science Award of Beijing Science and Technology Award (Second Prize) in 2024. His research has won the Best Paper Award of ACM Multimedia 2012 (the first time a Chinese university was the lead institution to receive this award; only 1 paper receives this award among 4 candidates), the Best Paper Award of IEEE Transactions on Multimedia 2023 (the sole winner that year), the Outstanding Doctoral Thesis Award of China Computer Federation (Rank 3 among 10 winners, out of 37 nominees in China), the Best Student Paper Award of MMM, and the Best Paper Award of ACM Multimedia HUMA Workshop. Key technologies won 1st place in the CVPR 2024 V3Det Challenge and Best Paper Award Finalist at ACM Multimedia 2025.

He has published over 190 academic papers (142 in conference proceedings, 48 in journals), including 74 publications ranked Class A by China Computer Federation (CCF-A), and authored two English monographs as first author. His Google Scholar citations are 5905 with an H-index of 40 (as of January 2026). He is an Associate Editor of IEEE TMM and Guest Editor of ACM TIST, JCST, and Future Generation Computer Systems. His research has been covered by prestigious technology media, including MIT Technology Review and Synced Review, becoming an important reference in the field.
