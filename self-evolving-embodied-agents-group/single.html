Building autonomous AI systems that learn, adapt, and evolve through interaction with the physical world. Advancing the frontier of embodied intelligence through multimodal foundation models and reinforcement learning.

Explore Research SEEA-R1 Project

Research Focus

We investigate how intelligent agents can autonomously improve their reasoning and behavior in complex, long-horizon embodied environments. Our work bridges multimodal foundation models, reinforcement learning, and robotic systems.

Embodied AI Foundation Models Self-Evolution
01

Tree-Structured RL

SEEA-R1 framework integrating Monte Carlo Tree Search with Group Relative Policy Optimization (GRPO) to convert sparse rewards into dense intermediate signals for multi-step reasoning.

02

Multimodal Reward Models

Multi-modal Generative Reward Models (MGRM) that generalize reward estimation across tasks and scenes, enabling autonomous adaptation without hand-crafted reward functions.

03

Simulation & Reality

Bridging the sim-to-real gap through advanced simulation infrastructure and synthetic data pipelines for scalable embodied intelligence training.

Featured Research

SEEA-R1: Tree-Structured Reinforcement Fine-Tuning

The first reinforcement fine-tuning framework designed for self-evolving embodied agents. Achieving 85.07% on ALFWorld benchmark through tree-based exploration and multimodal reward modeling.
